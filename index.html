About me

I am a graduate of engineering studies in the field of landscape architecture at the University of Warmia and Mazury in Olsztyn. The studies allowed me to develop my passion for creating space. I design gardens and green areas, I make professional 3D visualizations from 2019. During this time, I was able to take part in many amazing projects, both private and public.
I focus on creating spaces where greenery is an integral element of the designed architecture, and the space development design relates to the surrounding landscape. The selected material must survive in the most extreme conditions that a given place can provide, so I choose the vegetation based on the type of terrain, soil structure and the landscape surrounding the designed space.
I do not stop gaining new qualifications and continuous development, I willingly and actively participate in courses, lectures and trainings, as well as exhibitions and events devoted to landscape architecture.

Kamila Pankowska

Landscape architect

Experience

I gained experience during my studies, when I had the pleasure of designing space under the supervision of designers and contractors. I learned about design applications such as AutoCad, SketchUp, Lumion, Photoshop, CorelDraw. The experience gained in the field allows me to look at the landscape differently. After working in a design office, I moved to Norway, where I learn about the climate and local standards in landscape design and decoration.

Cracow, 2020

Naturalistic style garden
in Cracow

The main goal was to use the potential of the place with as little interference with nature as possible, so as to preserve its character. In the immediate vicinity of the house, minimalist geometric plantings have been introduced, which fits in with the modern shape of the house. In the further parts of the plantings, they become freer and more natural. Plants are characterized by good winter resistance and soil tolerance.

Cracow, 2021

Viewing pavilion in the park
in Cracow

The observation pavilion creates a safe and warm meeting place, offering a view of the park. It is located in a large group of trees, has a simple form, its main material is wood. Inside there is a long wooden bench. The wood was connected to each other in a traditional way, using wooden pegs. The pavilion is a solid structure, blending into the existing landscape, which gives guests the opportunity to reflect and contemplate the vast and rich landscape.
